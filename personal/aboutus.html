The Barton Reading & Spelling System

The Barton Reading & Spelling System is a structured, Orton-Gillingham-based program designed to help individuals with dyslexia and other reading difficulties improve their literacy skills. It is a systematic, explicit, and multisensory phonics-based program that teaches spelling, reading, and writing in a step-by-step manner.

Key Features of the Barton System

Multisensory Approach – Students learn through visual, auditory, and kinesthetic methods (hearing, seeing, and touching/moving). Explicit and Systematic Instruction – Concepts are broken down into small, manageable steps and taught directly. One-on-One Tutoring – The program is designed for tutors to work individually with students. Sequential and Cumulative – The system follows a structured order, ensuring that students master foundational skills before moving on. Focus on Phonemic Awareness – It starts by ensuring students can recognize and manipulate individual sounds (phonemes) before learning to read and spell words.

Barton System Structure

The program consists of 10 levels, progressing from basic phonemic awareness to advanced reading and spelling skills.

Level 1: Phonemic Awareness Level 2: Consonants & Short Vowels Level 3: Closed Syllables & Units Level 4: Multi-Syllable Words & Vowel Teams Level 5: Prefixes & Suffixes Level 6: Silent-E Words Level 7: Vowel-R & Advanced Vowel Teams Level 8: Advanced Prefixes & Suffixes Level 9: Greek Words Level 10: Latin Words

Who Can Benefit?

Children and adults with dyslexia Students struggling with spelling and reading Homeschoolers needing a structured literacy approach Teachers and tutors working with struggling readers
