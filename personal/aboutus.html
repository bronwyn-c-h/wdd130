The Barton Reading & Spelling System

The Barton Reading & Spelling System is a structured, Orton-Gillingham-based program designed to help individuals with dyslexia and other reading difficulties improve their literacy skills. It is a systematic, explicit, and multisensory phonics-based program that teaches spelling, reading, and writing in a step-by-step manner.

Key Features of the Barton System

  • Multisensory Approach – Students learn through visual, auditory, and kinesthetic methods.
  • Explicit and Systematic Instruction – Concepts are broken into small, manageable steps.
  • One-on-One Tutoring – Designed for tutors to work individually with students.
  • Sequential and Cumulative – Ensures mastery of foundational skills before progressing.
  • Focus on Phonemic Awareness – Emphasizes recognizing and manipulating individual sounds before learning to read and spell.

Barton System Structure

The program consists of 10 levels, progressing from basic phonemic awareness to advanced reading and spelling skills.

  • Level 1: Phonemic Awareness
  • Level 2: Consonants & Short Vowels
  • Level 3: Closed Syllables & Units
  • Level 4: Multi-Syllable Words & Vowel Teams
  • Level 5: Prefixes & Suffixes
  • Level 6: Silent-E Words
  • Level 7: Vowel-R & Advanced Vowel Teams
  • Level 8: Advanced Prefixes & Suffixes
  • Level 9: Greek Words
  • Level 10: Latin Words

Who Can Benefit?

  • Children and adults with dyslexia
  • Students struggling with spelling and reading
  • Homeschoolers needing a structured literacy approach
  • Teachers and tutors working with struggling readers
